Planet Reach

Epsilon Eridani II - Humanity's Military Keystone (2510-2552)

Overview

Reach is Earth's closest interstellar neighbor at 10.5 light-years, terraformed in 2362 and designated Epsilon Eridani II. With rich titanium deposits and stable climate zones, it evolved into the United Nations Space Command's primary shipyard and training center—home to the SPARTAN-II program, SWORD Base, and orbital Aszod shipyards.

Key Regions & Installations

  • Viery Territory: Rugged mountain ranges; site of Visegrád relay and initial Covenant incursion.
  • New Alexandria: Coastal megacity and economic hub, later a major evacuation corridor.
  • Aszod Shipbreaking Yards: Orbital elevator and frigate construction slips.
  • SWORD Base (Menachite Mountain): ONI research complex housing Cortana fragments.
  • Quezon Reservoir & Manassas Highlands: UNSC training grounds and airfields.

Historical Timeline

  1. 2362: Terraforming complete; first colonial wave arrives.
  2. 2517: Dr. Halsey selects Reach as clandestine site for the SPARTAN-II training at CASTLE Base.
  3. 2525-2551: Reach serves as UNSC fleet anchor during early Human-Covenant War.
  4. July 24 2552: Covenant stealth forces infiltrate Viery relay—start of Reach Campaign.
  5. August 14 2552: Supercarrier Long Night of Solace destroyed above Reach.
  6. August 30 2552: Covenant fleet begins vitrification (“glassing”) of the surface.
  7. September 2552: Reach officially declared lost; remaining UNSC survivors evacuated or unrecovered.

Strategic Importance to the UNSC

Reach's orbital yards produced over 60% of frontline frigates and carriers, while its military academies trained ODST and Spartan units. Its fall crippled humanity's fleet logistics and R&D capacity mere weeks before the Covenant discovered Earth.

Aftermath & Legacy

The near-total glassing rendered Reach uninhabitable, yet subterranean ONI caches (CASTLE Base) endured—later re-explored during Operation: WHITE GLOVE in 2553. Today Reach stands as a solemn memorial: a testament to Spartan sacrifice and a strategic lesson in the perils of centralized defense.
